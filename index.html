Camel

  1. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  2. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  3. Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals..
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animal.
  3. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time..

Chameleon

  1. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches..
  2. Their hands and feet have large toes that help them cling to branches..
  3. Almost halfof know species live in Madagascar.

Panda

  1. They have great camouflage for their environment..
  2. Cubs are well protected in their first month...
  3. Their eyes are different to normal bears.

Teddy

  1. The name teddy bear comes from former United States President Theodore Roosevelt.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906..
  3. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. Most have red-orange eyes.
  3. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.
